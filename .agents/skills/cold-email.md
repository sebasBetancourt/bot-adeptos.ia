# Cold Email Skill

Based on Corey Haines' B2B marketing framework.

**Core Principles for Cold Outreach:**
1. **Start with the Problem:** Do not pitch the product immediately. Focus on an acute pain point they are likely experiencing (e.g., losing revenue due to slow lead response).
2. **Personalization over Templates:** Open with an observation about their role or company.
3. **Agitate the Pain:** Quantify the cost of the problem if possible (e.g., $8,500/mo in revenue leaks).
4. **Specific Social Proof:** Introduce how you solved this exact problem for a similar company. Use numbers and clear transformations instead of vague claims.
5. **Low-friction CTA:** Do not ask for a 30-minute demo on a connection request. Offer an initial value exchange (e.g., a 15-min audit, dropping a useful resource, or a simple question like "Worth a quick chat?").
6. **Casual Tone (Peer-to-Peer):** Avoid "I hope this finds you well" and generic sales pitches. Speak like an equal. Use contractions. Keep it brief.
